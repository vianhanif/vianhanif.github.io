Since you are already planning to use LinkedIn for small updates, trying to force LinkedIn traffic to a personal blog, and then also trying to earn money on Medium, things can get messy if you don’t structure the flow correctly. LinkedIn actively suppresses posts containing external links because it wants to keep users on its own platform. [1] 
Here is an optimal ecosystem strategy designed to leverage all three channels, avoid search engine penalties, and maximize your potential to get paid.
------------------------------
## 1. The Strategy: Syndication (Republishing)
Do not split your content by putting some articles on your blog and completely different ones on Medium. Instead, write on your personal blog first, then cross-post (syndicate) the entire article to Medium a few days later. [2] 
Because Medium already has a built-in audience of paying members, importing your deep technical deep-dives (like your 9router or OpenCode pieces) to Medium gives you immediate access to a pool of tech-focused readers you do not have to hunt for yourself. [3] 
## 2. Protecting Your SEO: Use Canonical Links
If you copy and paste your blog post directly into Medium, Google will index Medium's version instead of yours because Medium has higher domain authority. To prevent Google from penalizing your personal website for duplicate content, you must use Medium's Advanced Settings to set a canonical link. [3, 4, 5] 
This tells Google: "My personal blog is the original source. Give my website the search traffic value, not Medium." [6, 7] 
How to set it:

   1. Import or paste your post draft into the Medium editor.
   2. Click the three-dot menu (...) in the top right and select More settings.
   3. Scroll down and click to expand Advanced Settings.
   4. Check the box for "This story was originally published elsewhere" and paste your exact vianhanif.github.io article URL. [5, 6] 

## 3. Fixing the LinkedIn Pipeline
LinkedIn’s algorithm penalizes external links in the main text box, meaning very few people will see your post if you just drop your blog link there. Use this workaround: [1] 

* The Post: Write a compelling, text-only text summary on LinkedIn covering a core takeaway from your latest project (e.g., "How I shrunk node_modules from 200MB to 2.5MB").
* The Call to Action: Write at the bottom of the post: "Full breakdown linked in the comments below."
* The Comments: Post two links in the first comment: One to your personal blog (for non-Medium users) and one to your Medium article (for Medium members).

------------------------------
## How to Get Paid From This Setup
Once you cross-post your technical articles to Medium and put them behind the paywall, Medium pays you based on Member Engagement Time (how long paying members spend reading your words). [8] 
Furthermore, Medium implemented critical updates to its algorithm that work highly in your favor for this exact strategy:

* The External Traffic Bonus: If non-members find your paywalled article via your LinkedIn comment link and end up signing up for a Medium membership within 30 days, Medium awards you a Conversion Bonus.
* SEO Slice: Medium splits a specific budget to reward articles that pull in organic traffic via search engines. [8, 9] 

## Eligibility Requirements to Start Earning
Note that Medium updated its rules to deter spam bots. Before you can hit the "monetize" button on your imported posts, your Medium profile must fulfill these baselines: [10] 

* You must have published at least 6 stories on the platform.
* Your account must be active for at least 3 months.
* You must reside in a [Stripe-supported country](https://stripe.com/global) to link your banking and tax info. [10, 11]
